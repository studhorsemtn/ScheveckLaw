The Scheveck Law Firm is a full-service law firm located in downtown Billings, MT. We focus our practice in the areas of personal injury, criminal defense, family law, bankruptcy, and oil & gas law. We are licensed to practice in all Montana State Courts and State Federal District Court.






Layne Scheveck's passion for a career in law began early in his undergraduate studies at Dakota Wesleyan University, where he interned at Morgan Theeler, PLLC. He graduated from DWU in 2010 with a bachelor's degreee in business with an emphasis in accounting and entreprenueurship. During his legal studies at Thomas M. Cooley Law School in Grand Rapids, Michigan, he concentrated his time with seminars in family law.

Prior to starting Scheveck Law Firm, Layne worked at the Montana Public Defender's Office, where he represented defendants with misdemeanors and felonies. He also worked as a private attorney, where he represented clients for various cases, such as motorcycle personal injury, divorce, parenting plans, and DUI defense. He focuses on personal injury with concentration in motorcycle accidents, contract disputes, family law, and criminal law, in addition to landlord tenant law,

Layne spends his free time snowmobiling through the Beartooth Mountains, exploring the beautiful state of Montana, while riding his motorcycle, and discovering new hobbies.







Lyndon Scheveck grew up in Billings, Montana. He is a persistent litigator and brings a unique perspective to the law. He strongly believes in the constitution and that everyone has a right to be heard and be judged in front of their peers.

He graduated from Dakota Wesleyan University in Mitchell, South Dakota where he earned his Bachelors degree in Business Administration and his Masters degree in Education. Mr. Scheveck later went to get his Juris Doctorate from Appalachian School Law in Grundy, Virginia, where he was a member of the Trial Advocacy Program and earned a certificate in natural resources. During law school he worked at the Yellowstone County Courthouse and worked as a Landman for oil companies.

Lyndon is currently in the process of being licensed in the State of North Dakota and is waiting for the results of the Montan State Bar.

When Lyndon is not in his office, you can always find him coaching at the local gym. You can also find him on one of Montana's many rivers fly fishing.
